This is my reflection Homework on the graphic novel

When reading “Memunato The distant patient ‘Birth in my village’,” I felt that it was sad, because this story takes place in 2020 and I had no idea people in these areas didn’t have access to doctors to help women give birth. I thought people only had childbirth issues in the past and that it was over. Reading this showed me how much ignorance I had towards people in other areas because I didn’t know that they were struggling. It’s scary that she lost her child, and couldn’t urinate for 3 days. If that happened to me I would freak out because I think kids are really cute and because in the comic you can see how much she is hurting and struggling. I’m glad some people found her to get treatment, and I smiled when she did give birth to a baby later in the future. I feel like this story has a sense of hierarchy when it shows her lying in bed and we are looking from the top to show that she is afraid and feels powerless, and it shows how she thought she was alone in this situation for so long.

When reading “Heela:the schoolgirl ‘my stolen future’ ” I felt that it was scary that she was taken advantage of, and I feel like she also should’ve stood up for herself when she was being sexually harassed. She had a right to say no. I also feel that it was scary because she was so young and it’s just wrong that the father of the baby just fled. I’m glad she told her grandma in the end and how she is in a safe place with her dad and her baby now. I also never heard of this disease called “Ebola”, and it’s scary how in 2020 people are still getting sick with diseases that are hard to cure, and then covid hits soon after. I feel like Heela’s story uses lots of color choices in her comic. There’s a colorful grid in the third image and their school uniforms have a green and yellow color.

When reading “Kadiatu: The grieving mother ‘The Devil At My Door’ ”, I feel like it’s scary and sad how the mom went through so much in her life before covid. I also feel like they should stop seeing the traditional healer because he makes the mother feel worse and heightens her fear based on what he says. I’m glad they’re doing better today, but this comic showed how much covid has changed the world a lot and possibly made lives worse for some. I feel like Kadiatu’s story has a sense of hierarchy to show that she is afraid and scared.

When reading “Tetteh: the prisoner ‘my punishment’, It was sad and really shows the struggles that women go through in life. The comic showed me that there are actually people out there struggling and afraid. It is also sad that Tetteh has not seen her son for over a year, and all her actions were to protect him. I feel like Tetteh’s story also has hierarchy to show how the husband was abusive and the fear she felt trying to protect herself and her baby.

Overall when reading these comics it shows the internal struggles of women and how life is scary as you grow older. You have to make sacrifices and the right decisions.
